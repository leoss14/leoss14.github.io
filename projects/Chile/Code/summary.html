Chile Mineral Supply Chain Pipeline

A six-part Python/Jupyter pipeline that constructs a directed supply chain network for Chilean mining, linking individual extraction sites to processing facilities, export ports, and destination countries using official Chilean and international trade statistics.

6 Notebooks Python · Pandas · NumPy 2005–2024 Cu · Mo · Li · Au · Ag · Fe · Zn

Overview

What the Pipeline Does

The pipeline takes raw, heterogeneous data from multiple Chilean government and international trade sources and assembles them into a single, validated network representation of Chile's mineral supply chain. Each run produces two core outputs: an inventory of facilities (mines, concentrators, smelters, refineries, ports) with production figures attached, and an edge table encoding directed material flows between those facilities.

The work is divided into six sequential notebooks, each saving an intermediate state file (_pipeline_state_N.pkl) that the next notebook loads. This avoids reprocessing expensive steps and allows any part to be debugged or rerun in isolation.

Inputs

Data Sources

Four distinct sources feed the pipeline. They differ in granularity, update frequency, and the layer of the supply chain they cover.

COCHILCO Production Statistics

Chile's copper commission (Comisión Chilena del Cobre) publishes company-level annual production tables for copper and molybdenum. This is the primary source for matching production volumes to individual facilities.

COCHILCO Anuario (Yearbook)

A companion publication with regional and national aggregate figures for all minerals including gold, silver, iron, and zinc, as well as tables of export quantities by destination country. Read directly from Excel sheets.

Sernageomin Inventory

The Chilean geological survey (Servicio Nacional de Geología y Minería) maintains a georeferenced register of all mining facilities with facility type, operator, coordinates, and reserve/resource tonnages. Used as the base inventory throughout.

Aduanas & COMTRADE

Customs agency (Aduanas) port-level export records (salidas_2024_clean.csv) assign shipment volumes to specific Chilean ports. UN COMTRADE data provides national-level export figures used to cross-validate the Aduanas port shares.

Terminology

Key Terms and Methods

Several domain-specific terms and modeling choices appear throughout the notebooks. The glossary below covers the most important ones for readers who have not previously worked with Chilean mining data.

SX-EW
Solvent extraction / electrowinning. A hydrometallurgical process that leaches copper from oxide ores and deposits it directly as copper cathode, bypassing the concentrator-smelter route used for sulphide ores.
Chain stage
A label applied to each facility indicating where it sits in the supply chain: extraction → concentration → smelting → refining, or the parallel sx_ew pathway.
Edge table
A tabular representation of directed material flows. Each row describes a link from one facility (FROM_NAME) to another (TO_NAME), with the commodity, product form, and volume attached.
COCHILCO kMT
Production volumes reported by COCHILCO in thousands of metric tonnes of fine metal content. Column names follow the pattern COCHILCO_CU_2024_KMT.
Haversine distance
A formula that computes the great-circle distance between two points on the Earth's surface given their latitude and longitude. Used here to measure mine-to-port distances without requiring a road network.
Resource weighting
When company-level data is unavailable (Au, Ag, Fe, Zn), a mine's share of a regional production total is estimated in proportion to its declared reserve or resource tonnage from Sernageomin, under the assumption that larger deposits contribute more to regional output.
Port share (Aduanas)
The fraction of a product type's exports that passed through each Chilean port in 2024, derived from customs declaration records. Applied to allocate export volumes to specific port nodes in the network.
Pipeline state (.pkl)
A Python pickle file containing the full working state (inventory DataFrame, edge table, and supporting dictionaries) at the end of each notebook. Allows subsequent notebooks to resume without rerunning earlier steps.

Outputs

What the Pipeline Produces

Facility Inventory A georeferenced table of Chilean mining facilities (mines, concentrators, smelters, refineries, ports) with operator names, chain stage labels, product forms, and annual production figures attached for copper, molybdenum, lithium, gold, silver, iron, and zinc for the period 2005–2024.
Supply Chain Edge Table A directed graph edge list linking each facility to its downstream counterpart, annotated with commodity, product form, and volume. The full chain runs from extraction sites through processing nodes to export ports and, via destination shares, to receiving countries.
Distance Validation Report A comparison of modelled port-routing against observed customs-derived port assignments, expressed as a share of total copper production volume. Provides a quantitative indication of how well the spatial assumptions in the network hold against real shipment data.
