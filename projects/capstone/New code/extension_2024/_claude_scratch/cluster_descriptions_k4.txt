================================================================================
RESOURCE-RICH CLUSTERS (k=4)
================================================================================

Threshold: mean wide_resource_share > 0.40 over 1995-2023
Sample: 64 countries
Features (7): coal_share, crude_oil_share, refined_oil_share, gas_share,
              ores_share, base_metals_share, precious_share
Pipeline: log1p(features) -> PCA(2) -> KMeans(k=4), ensembled across M=5 MICE
          imputations via co-membership consensus.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLUSTER 0 -- MIXED EXTRACTIVES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
N = 20 countries (aggregate snapshot)
Median shares: ores 10.0%, precious 7.7%, base_metals 3.8%, refined_oil 2.2%

Diversified resource economies that span multiple categories without being
dominated by any single one. Mining is the prevailing theme but composition
varies: some have meaningful precious or fuel side-income (PNG with gas,
MNG with coal, BOL with gas, MRT with crude oil), others are mining-focused
with a developed manufacturing sector overlay.

Anchor countries: MNG, PER, PNG, BOL, MRT
Stable members: ARM, EGY, IDN, JAM, LAO, MMR, NER, RWA, SDN, SEN, SLB,
                TGO, TTO, ZAF, ZWE

Notable trajectories:
  GHA: precious cluster (2010) -> mixed (2023) after Jubilee oil field
  MOZ: base metals (2010) -> mixed in 2023 with coal+gas emerging

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLUSTER 1 -- HYDROCARBON PETROSTATES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
N = 23 countries (aggregate snapshot)
Median shares: crude_oil 59.3%, refined_oil 5.7%, gas 3.4%, base_metals 1.0%

Crude-oil-dominant exporters. Spans classical Gulf petrostates, African oil
exporters, Latin American oil economies, and post-Soviet petrostates. Some
have meaningful gas (QAT, DZA, RUS, AZE) or refining (KWT, VEN) but crude
oil is the unifying axis. At k=5 this cluster splits into pure-crude
(SAU/IRQ/AGO/NGA) and diversified-hydrocarbon (QAT/RUS/KAZ/ARE) groups.

Anchor countries: SAU, IRQ, AGO, NGA, KWT, RUS, QAT, AZE
Stable members: ARE, CMR, COG, COL, DZA, ECU, GAB, IRN, LBY, OMN, SYR,
                TLS, VEN, YEM

Notable trajectories:
  GUY: enters cluster in 2023 (offshore ExxonMobil discovery, first oil 2019)
  YEM: exits cluster in 2023 (war collapsed oil infrastructure)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLUSTER 2 -- BASE METALS EXPORTERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
N = 9 countries (aggregate snapshot)
Median shares: base_metals 31.8%, ores 4.7%, refined_oil 1.2%, precious 1.1%

Copper, iron, aluminum, and other industrial-metal exporters. Smaller but
tightly defined group. ZMB (Zambia, 72% copper) and COD (DR Congo, 59%
base metals from copper-cobalt) are the archetypes. CHL (Chile) sits in
this cluster despite Chile's mining narrative because copper is processed
into wirebar and refined copper rather than exported as ore.

Anchor countries: ZMB, COD, CHL, MOZ
Stable members: BHR (refined oil edge case), BTN, MNE, TJK, UKR

Notable trajectories:
  MNG: drifts here in 2023 due to coal share growing (58% by 2023),
       though this is really a coal story not a base metals one;
       k=6 might separate it. Acceptable approximation at k=4.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLUSTER 3 -- PRECIOUS METALS AND STONES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
N = 12 countries (aggregate snapshot)
Median shares: precious 34.9%, ores 1.7%, base_metals 1.5%, refined_oil 0.8%

Gold and diamond exporters, predominantly West African. BWA (Botswana)
dominates the cluster centroid with 81% precious. CAF (Central African
Republic) is at 64% precious in 1995 and stable.

Anchor countries: BWA, MLI, GHA, GIN, BFA, CAF
Stable members: GUY (until 2023 oil pulled it out), KGZ, NAM, SUR,
                TZA, UZB

Notable trajectories:
  GHA: exits the cluster in 2023 (Jubilee oil; see Cluster 0)
  YEM: enters in 2023 (oil collapse leaving artisanal gold)

================================================================================
INTERPRETATION NOTES FOR WRITEUP
================================================================================

1. The 4-cluster taxonomy is stable across 1995/2010/2023 snapshots. Most
   countries stay in the same cluster; transitions reflect real economic
   shifts (new oil discoveries, war-induced collapse, LNG buildouts).

2. The k=4 partition gives a clean cross-time comparison. k=5 splits the
   petrostate cluster into pure-crude vs. diversified-hydrocarbon, which is
   the analytical payoff of disaggregating HS27 but only meaningfully visible
   in the 2010 and 2023 snapshots where N is large enough.

3. Cluster boundaries are determined in 2D PCA space, not in the 7-dim
   feature space. This means visual closeness on the PC1-PC2 plane is
   what generates cluster membership; the underlying feature space has
   more structure that is partly compressed by the projection.

4. The clustering is ensembled across M=5 MICE imputations via co-membership
   consensus, then agglomeratively clustered on (1 - co_membership) for the
   final assignment. Stability scores per country are in
   Master_v2_clusters_RR_silhouette.csv.
